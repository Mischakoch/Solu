Zero Export Management
for Reduced Electricity Bills

Increase your self-consumption


Grid electricity prices are constantly on the rise.

This situation motivates installation of PV systems that allow business and property owners to reduce consumption from the grid during the day. However, some local regulations in Cyprus may or may not allow exporting power to the grid, or limit the amount of power that can be exported. Therefore, an energy management system supports the installation of PV systems where no export is permitted or where export power is limited or governed through regulation.


The Solutronic zero export Management solution offers an export limitation option (Feed-in Limitation/zero export), integrated in the Solutronic inverter firmware, which dynamically adjusts PV power production. This allows system owners to install PV systems of any size, while ensuring the power exported to the grid does not exceed the limitation.

Solutronic Zero Export and Feed-in Limitation


• Export limitation integrated into the inverter firmware - install only an energy meter

• Fast Response Time - ensures that even with rapid changes in load consumption and PV production, the exported power does not exceed the limit

• Failsafe Operation - the operation is designed to limit the export of power to the grid so it does not exceed the preconfigured limit under any fault


Use the Solutronic Inverter as your Energy Manager


• Export limit can be configured by Solutronic if required by a local utility provider, and can be locked after set up to prevent unauthorised configuration changes

• In a multi-inverter system, one inverter will serve as the energy manager

• Installed Solutronic inverters can be firmware upgraded with the export limitation option

How it works...

Electrical energy is generated from Solutronic's IFRISol crystalline photovoltaic panels, flowing to the Solutronic SolPlus Inverter.

The inverter converts the generated DC current to AC, enabling  direct use by household appliances or commercial business appliances.

Surplus energy then  flows to the Solutronic Grid Manager, which then redirects the flow back to the inverter.
 
A Solar Log optimises production, consumption and feed-in from the national grid. 

zero energy is exported to the national grid, YET when  supply is insufficient for the load required, 
the grid responds with immediate feed-in power.
