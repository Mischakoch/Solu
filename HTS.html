High Temperature Storage [HTS]

Integration

How do we integrate high temperature storage [HTS] into a network of E Car charging stations, wind parks, local solar plants, battery storage, industrial and large power plants, as well as accomodate grid disturbances, fluctuating power supply with network protection to limit grid disturbance?

Main Applications

Utilities

Balancing and time shifting to optimise demand

Quick voltage - frequency and reactive power plants

Store excessive energy from wind and PV power plants

Peak load shaving

Improve availability of renewable energies

Improve availability of renewable energies, supply control energy and provide base load operation possibilities for renewable energies

Avoid expansion of long distance electricity supplies

Absorb waste heat, e.g. from waste incineration plants

Provide local energy for EV chargers without increasing existing energy supply. Up to 100 EV (electric vehicle) chargers can be connected to one HTS power storage unit.

Specification of HTS

High Temperature Storage
  
  • Scalable storage sizes - 5MWh up to 100MWh
  • Long term stability - 50 years at 10 full cycles per day
  • Safety - low bat pressure max. 6 bar, operation without liquids
  • Generator power - cascadeable  from 100kW up to 10MW
  • Response times - within seconds of operation
  • Storage temperature - 500 - 1200 0C (700 0C temperature difference) 
  • Storage period: from seconds to 30 days (losses 1% per day)
  • Efficiency: 40 – 50% electric energy, 40 – 50% thermal energy 
  • Economic efficiency: cost only 1/3 compared with battery storage (lithium) 
  • Dimensions of 5MWh storage: diameter 4.5 m, Additional space for turbine, recuperator and generator required
  • Placement: Integration surface or sub (underground)

Function

Functional Description:
• Storage medium for the electrical energy or process heat is a special stone which is compressed and filled in the storage tank
• The storage tank is made of steel for the 5MWh and 10MWh solutions, beyond that concrete is used (up to 100MWh)
• The storage tank is filled with the stones. Special isolation material mounted on the outside, so that reflective heat outside is less than 30  degrees.
• The stones are heated up with E-rods (similar to a water boiler) or compressed hot air (from waste plants). Temperatures up to 1200 °C can. be reached. 

Energy Distribution

Efficiency
• A recuperator is used to increase the efficiency
• Hot air which is exhausted from the turbine will be captured and re-used in the storage
• Thermal energy from the storage can be physically divided up into „Exergy“ and „Anergy“
• Total energy losses are 
Turbine 3.43%, 
Gearing 1.97%, 
Compressor 3.26%, 
Recuperator 1.11%
Total losses approx. 10%!
• The recuperator regains the energy losses and contributes with exergy to the high efficiency of the storage system

Principle Schematic

Operation:
• Process air is compressed
• Compressed air flows through the solid state storage and is heated up. This causes an expansion of the air which then drives the turbine.
• The turbine is using the storage instead of a "burning chamber “ to turn.
• The turbine itself is connected through a gearing with the generator which produces electric energy.
• In order to start the turbine and generator, a starter motor has to be used (similar to a helicopter turbine)

About HTS

HTS Power Storage GmbH was founded May 3rd, 2016 in Bramberg am Hochkönig Austria.
Target of the company is to develop, produce, install and integrate High Temperature Storage Systems.
Our goal is to build an energy storage system which is sustainable, can store excessive energy (process heat and electrical  energy), releases the energy again in the form of thermal and electric power to integrate renewable energy, making it able meet base load demands.
